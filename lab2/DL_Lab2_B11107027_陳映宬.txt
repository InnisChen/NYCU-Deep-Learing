Implementation Highlights:
Two encoder-decoder architectures implemented from scratch.
UNet: valid convolutions (no padding), center-crop skip connections, transposed conv upsampling, 1x1 output conv.
ResNet34+UNet: ResNet34 encoder, Bridge module for multi-scale feature fusion, UNet decoder with CBAM attention per block.
Loss: BCE + Soft Dice (50/50) to prevent all-background collapse.
AMP mixed precision + gradient clipping (max_norm=20).

Data Preprocessing Strategy:
Images resized to 384x384. Trimap to binary: value 1->foreground(1), values 2,3->background(0).
Train augmentations: Random HFlip, VFlip, Rotation (+-30 deg), ColorJitter.
ImageNet normalization applied.
Reflection padding (+94px per side) to compensate UNet valid convolution shrinkage.

Training Strategy:
Optimizer: AdamW (lr=1e-4, weight_decay=1e-4).
Scheduler: CosineAnnealingLR (T_max=200, eta_min=1e-6).
Batch size: 32, Epochs: 200. Best model saved by validation Dice.

Key Observations:
Three LR schedulers were tested. ReduceLROnPlateau (no lower bound) decayed LR too aggressively, causing the model to stop learning. MultiStepLR (x0.5 at epochs 50/100/150) was more stable but suboptimal.
CosineAnnealingLR (final choice): smooth decay to eta_min=1e-6 avoids premature collapse and yields the best validation Dice.

Comparison: UNet vs ResNet34_UNet:
UNet uses convolutions to shrink dimensions, requiring reflection padding. Simple and effective but limited in representational capacity.
ResNet34+UNet: residual connections improve gradient flow; CBAM enhances decoder feature selection; Bridge fuses multi-scale features. Achieves higher Dice score due to stronger encoder representation.
